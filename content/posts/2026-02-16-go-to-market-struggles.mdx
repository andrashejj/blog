At NBK Labs, the playbook's battle-tested across projects: launch B2C first‚Äîland real users, prove the engine hums. Feedback's strong, retention sticks. Then strip the consumer UI, repackage the platform as B2B2C SaaS, sell to partners with built-in audiences.

What works: validation fast, tech modular. What doesn't: **nobody buys quick**. LOIs pile up, contracts evaporate. Distribution's the eternal grind‚Äîhere's what I'm learning.

## B2C: Quick proof, quick plateau

B2C ships lean: users try, love it, share a bit. Organic spikes from launches, threads.

Then stall. No channel = no scale. "Go viral" sounds good, delivers nothing repeatable. Chased collabs, posts‚Äîlifts fade without ad muscle or luck.

Works: Proves product-market fit cheap. Doesn't: Sustainable growth solo.

<Divider />

## B2B2C: Borrow their flywheel

Keep B2C live as demo. Core play: white-label the APIs/dashboard to partners (apps, services with users).

- **B2C app**: User love proof.
- **Platform**: Embeddable scale.
- **Partners**: Their distro = ours.

Modular stacks make it plug-and-play. Evolution from past runs‚Äîsmarter than pure B2C roulette.

## B2B sales: The "maybe" marathon

B2C ghosts outright. B2B? Polite eternity.

25+ convos: intros, colds, inbounds. Champions: "Perfect stack fit." LOIs signed. Pilots floated.

Closes? Zero.

The script:
> "Intriguing."  
> "Pilot?"  
> "Internal sync."  
> "Next quarter."

LOIs = morale boost. Contracts = runway.

### Gauntlet lived 15x

1. Outreach.
2. Pain check.
3. Enthusiasm spike.
4. Stakeholders.
5. Pilot (paid beats free).
6. Legal/security.
7. Procurement.
8. **Contract**.

One snag, stalled.
