In the [first post](/blog/2026/04/balaton-cottage-renovation) I described the vision for our Balaton hilltop cottage — what we want to build, why, and what makes the property special. This post goes deeper: it's the visual reference library we've been building with my wife Dori. Room by room, image by image, this is the design language we're drawn to.

Think of it as a moodboard made public. Every image here represents something specific we want to capture — a material, a layout idea, a feeling. Not everything will end up in the final design, but together they paint a clear picture of the direction.

The aesthetic in one line: **wabi-sabi meets Japandi, grounded in the Hungarian countryside.** Natural materials, warm neutrals, built-in everything, nothing precious.

---

## Kitchen

The kitchen is being completely reoriented to face outward — toward the terrace and garden. A new wall opening connects it visually and physically to the living room. The kitchen island becomes the hub: cooking, prep, casual seating, and storage all in one generous piece.

We're drawn to natural wood cabinetry with rattan or cane-panelled doors, open shelves flanking the window, and a palette that stays warm and earthy. No glossy surfaces, no clinical white. When you're cooking here, you should feel like you're in a country kitchen that happens to be beautifully designed.

## Living room

The living room is really three zones in one: a couch/window seat area for reading and lounging, an armchair corner for quieter conversation, and a dining area anchored by the green tile stove. They share the same warm material palette but each has its own character.

### Couch & window seat area

The dream is a built-in window seat nook or daybed — somewhere between a reading alcove and a sofa. Bookshelves flank the seating, cushions are firm and washable, and the whole thing invites you to curl up with a book or a glass of wine.

## The palette, in summary

Across all these rooms, a few threads keep recurring. The colour palette stays within warm neutrals — cream, sand, honey wood — with sage green and terracotta as the two accent colours. Materials are natural and tactile: reclaimed wood, linen, lime plaster, rattan, natural stone. Built-in furniture wherever possible. Ambient, indirect lighting throughout. And nothing — absolutely nothing — that makes you afraid to touch it.
