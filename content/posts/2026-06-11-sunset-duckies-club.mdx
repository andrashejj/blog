The club happened by accident, which is the only reason it has a chance.

There was a board. Then there were versions of a logo. The kids voted, changed their minds, defended bad options, killed some good ones, and helped push the duck toward its final shape. Then there were stickers. The stickers had to go somewhere, and the obvious place was on the boards of the kids who had helped choose them.

That is enough. Children do not need bylaws before they are allowed to belong to something.

## Who's in it

<ImageFloat src="/images/board-project/IMG_4998.jpeg" alt="Noah and one of the shapers talking over the board in the bay" side="right" width="44%">

Noah is the person who drew the first duck, which gives him more authority than any adult title would. The other kids helped choose what that duck became, which matters just as much for a club. I am the one who carries things and takes too many photos. Daniel Kux, who taught him through the build, is obviously in. Bron, Mark, Theila, and Robyn helped around the project and got pulled in too.

</ImageFloat>

Then there are the kids at the break who wanted a sticker and got one. They did not earn it. There is no initiation. The whole membership requirement is that you surf there and think the duck is funny.

That is the right bar for a club of eight-year-olds.

## What it's for

Nothing, officially. That is the part I want to protect.

No fees. No schedule. No parent committee. No WhatsApp group slowly becoming a logistics swamp. Just a name, a sticker, and the loose understanding that if you have one on your board, you are part of the thing.

Names do something to children. A kid who is "just surfing" and a kid who is in a tiny invented surf club are the same kid, except one of them feels watched by a story. Sometimes that is enough to make him paddle a little harder.

## Where it goes

I do not want to overbuild this. The fastest way to kill a kid's club is for adults to notice it is working and improve it into something unbearable.

So the plan is to do almost nothing on purpose. Print more stickers when we run out. Show up. Run the small contest again if the kids still care. Let Noah decide what it is for as long as he wants to.

If it fades by next year, fine. A sticker gave a few kids at Tamarin the feeling of being a crew for one season. That is already more than it needed to do.
