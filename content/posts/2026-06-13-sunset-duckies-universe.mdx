We set out to build a surfboard. That was the whole plan.

The duck came later. Then the logo versions. Then the stickers. Then other kids wanted the same sticker on their boards. Then the small club had a name before anyone had decided it was a club.

This page is just a map so the pieces don't sit around as separate half-stories.

- [The board](/blog/sunset-duckies-board): the three-month build at Santosha, from blank foam to finished 5'6" shortboard.
- [The duck](/blog/sunset-duckies-brand): how Noah's pencil sketch became artwork for the board through versions, votes, and arguments about what looked right.
- [The stickers and shirts](/blog/sunset-duckies-merch): what happened when the drawing left the board and started showing up on other kids.
- [The club](/blog/sunset-duckies-club): a few children at Tamarin with the same sticker and no adult structure around it.
- [The first heat](/blog/sunset-duckies-competition): the board's first real test in the water.

## How it grew

The order matters.

If we'd started by saying we were launching a small surf brand for a group of kids, I hope someone would have stopped us. But that's not what happened. We started with a board. The board needed artwork. The artwork went through the kids. The artwork made good stickers. The stickers went onto other boards. The other boards made the kids look like a crew. The crew needed a day in the water.

At each step, the next thing was only a little ridiculous. That's how most projects get away from you.
