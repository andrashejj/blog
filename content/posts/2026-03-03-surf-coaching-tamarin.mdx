We moved to Mauritius roughly six months ago and it's been amazing. The ocean, the light, the pace of life — all of it. But one of the things that is definitely _not_ amazing is the service, the professionalism, the timing. I look at it from my "Swiss" perspective, being used to immaculate client focus and service, and I just see opportunities left and right. Unfortunately I already have too many things on my plate :)

But there's one thing I just _have_ to do: organize proper surf coaching for Noah, Lara and friends.

## More Than Just Catching Waves

It came to me slowly. The kids surf almost every day, but they're not really progressing. They paddle out, catch some whitewater, wipe out, paddle back, repeat. It's fun — but it's not _training_.

And then I realized: there really isn't any professional coaching here. Not just for surfing — generally. You _can_ book one-hour sessions with someone who shows you how to stand on a board. But that's not coaching.

Coaching, for me, means you have a **long-term plan** and **long-term goals**. You train when it's raining. You train when the waves are flat. And the training does not consist mainly of "the thing" but mainly of mobility, strength, coordination, and lots of other base exercises. The actual surfing is maybe 30% of the session. The other 70% is what _makes_ the surfing better.

Think about any serious sports program in Europe — football, tennis, swimming. Kids don't just play matches or swim laps for two hours. They warm up, they do coordination drills, they build strength, they learn theory. The sport-specific practice is built on top of a solid athletic foundation.

That's what I want for our kids.

## The Plan

So we're doing this now. Twice a week, rain or shine, at **Tamarin Bay**. The current schedule is **Tuesday and Wednesday, 17:00 to 19:00** — but we'll see how it goes and adjust as needed.

The group is Noah, Lara, and a handful of friends — roughly ages 8 to 12. Small enough to give everyone attention, big enough to make the team exercises and games fun.

Each session follows a structured two-hour plan:

1. **Team Warmup** — run along the beach, then a team game to get energy up
2. **Mobility & Coordination** — dynamic stretching, balance work, agility drills
3. **Strength & Conditioning** — partner exercises, core work, surf-specific movements
4. **Paddling & Water Skills** — technique drills, duck dives, sprint paddles, wave reading
5. **Surfing** — catching waves with a specific technique focus, plus free surf with coaching feedback
6. **Theory** — a short daily topic: tides, safety, board types, surf etiquette
7. **Cool-down & Stretching** — static stretches, breathing, session reflection
