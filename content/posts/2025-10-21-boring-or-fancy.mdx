This question keeps following me: do I pick boring or fancy work?

I keep reframing it, but it always comes back to motivation. Why am I saying yes to this thing right now?

I used to chase these in sequence.

First challenge, so I could learn fast.
Then control, because I wanted to shape decisions.
Then money, because after enough years you stop pretending compensation does not matter.

Now it is less idealistic and more practical.

I do not need every project to be sexy. I can do boring work if the team is strong and the upside is real. I can also do chaotic frontier work, but I now respect the energy cost way more than before.

My best zone is usually boring work with one hard edge. Enough discomfort to grow, not so much chaos that everything feels like a fire.

I am seeing both extremes right now.

At Motif, we are building the first independent wealth advisor, agentic and working only for you. It is exciting, but technically brutal. Some problems feel like pure R&D. You solve one hard thing, then two new unknowns show up.

In parallel, I have a tech leadership mandate at MCO (My Compliance Office). By startup stereotype, this is "boring". And yet the business performs really well, people execute, and the economics are healthy.

So if you ask me for a final conclusion, I still do not have one.

Fancy work can drain you fast. It is stimulating, but often fragmented, and flow is hard to keep.

Boring work can also trap you. You can coast, do just enough, and slowly stop pushing.

Right now, my answer is to run both lanes on purpose:
