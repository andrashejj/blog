§ 01

The role

Principal Engineer on MCO's internal platform team. You build the developer platform engineers work in, the testing platform code runs through, and the docker image promoted up to production. Senior IC. No direct reports.

You've built things end-to-end. Backend services, CI, observability, deployment. You've shipped infrastructure to production at scale and supported it there.

You partner closely with the separate AI Core team on integrating their agentic tooling (APS, Implementation Agent, evals) into the developer and testing platforms.

Two postures.

Posture 01 · with the platform
Builder

You build and evolve the developer platform, the testing platform, and the deployable docker image. CI/CD, k8s, observability, IDE integration, container hygiene. Up to and including DEV. Operations runs PROD; you sit as 3rd-level support.

Posture 02 · with the strategic work
Drive a major project

SHIP IT (dockerization), k8s and containers for prod, or a similar strategic initiative. You take primary ownership of one and drive it under the Founding Engineer's leadership.

§ 02

This role is not

  • Not Operations. Build up to DEV, hand over for PROD, sit as 3rd-level support.
  • Not embedded in scrum teams. The team is 100% focused on the platform; you are not 50% embedded.
  • Not a delivery role. Delivery is owned by scrum teams.
  • Not a manager. No direct reports. The Founding Engineer leads the team.
  • Not an architect for the monolith. Architecture sits with scrum team Architects and the Engineering Director.
§ 03

Mandate

01
Developer platform

Local and cloud dev environments, IDE setup, agent tooling integration. The daily-driver toolchain.

02
Testing platform

CI, eval harness, test environments through DEV. Pipeline up to and including DEV; PROD is Operations.

03
The deployable artifact

A docker image built once and promoted up the chain (DEV → STAGE → PROD) without modification. k8s orchestration, repeatable deploys.

Operations runs PROD. AI Core remains a separate team and owns the agentic tooling layer; you integrate it.

§ 04

Key responsibilities

  • Build the developer platform: dev environments, IDE setup, agent tooling integration
  • Build the testing platform: CI, eval harness, test environments through DEV
  • Build and own the deployable artifact: docker image, k8s orchestration, repeatable deploys
  • Drive one strategic platform project as primary owner (SHIP IT, k8s for prod, or similar)
  • Partner with AI Core on integrating their agentic tooling
  • 3rd-level support on platform escalations

§ 07

What success looks like

30 days
Met the team, the AI Core team, and key scrum module Architects. Picked up your strategic project and scoped the first deliverable. Made at least one concrete platform improvement (CI fix, observability default, k8s template).
90 days
Strategic project moving with clear milestones. Platform contributions visible in CI / observability / k8s. AI Core partnership rhythm established. Team running in scrum alongside you.
End of Phase 1 (6 months)
Your strategic project landed in production (or close to it). Platform measurably better on team-relevant metrics. Trusted name across engineering for platform questions.
