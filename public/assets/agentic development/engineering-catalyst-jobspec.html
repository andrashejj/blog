§ 01

The role

Founding engineer for MCO's internal platform team. You own the developer platform engineers work in, the testing platform code runs through, and the docker image that gets promoted up to production. Director-level. Hands-on. Manages 5–7 ICs.

You came up in tech product startups. You wrote the first eval suite, set up the first CI, ran the first incident postmortem. You've personally built a DevOps stack end-to-end, not just led a team that did.

You partner closely with the separate AI Core team on integrating their agentic tooling (APS, Implementation Agent, evals) into the developer and testing platforms. You don't own their work; they do.

Three postures.

Posture 01 · with the team
Hands-on lead

You inherit the existing DevOps team (5–7 engineers) and hire Principal Engineer backfill. Line-manage by doing the work alongside them. The team doesn't currently run in scrum. They will, within 90 days, because you'll have taught them.

Posture 02 · with the platform
Builder

You build and evolve the developer platform, the testing platform, and the deployable docker image. CI/CD, k8s, observability, IDE integration, container hygiene. Up to and including DEV. Operations runs PROD; you stay 3rd-level.

Posture 03 · with the strategic work
SHIP IT and k8s for prod

SHIP IT (dockerization) and k8s/containers for prod are the headline projects. You drive them under your own leadership; the team delivers; you ship.

§ 02

This role is not

  • Not Operations. Build up to DEV, hand over for PROD, sit as 3rd-level support.
  • Not an embedded coaching role. The team is 100% focused on the platform; it is not 50% embedded in scrum teams.
  • Not a governance function. Red team, certification, methods are run by the Engineering Director and Architects. You support them with tooling when useful, but you don't own them.
  • Not a corporate platform leader. We're not looking for someone who came up in a 1000-engineer org and specialized in one slice of it for a decade. We want someone who has personally done every job on this team at some point in their career.
  • Not a PMO. No status reports for a living.
  • Not a delegate-only manager. Half your time is hands-on engineering. If you stop coding, you stop being qualified for this role.
  • Not a delivery manager. Delivery is owned by scrum teams.
  • Not an architect for the monolith. Architecture sits with scrum team Architects and the Engineering Director.
§ 03

Mandate

01
Developer platform

Local and cloud dev environments, IDE setup, agent tooling integration. The daily-driver toolchain engineers work in.

02
Testing platform

CI, eval harness, test environments through DEV. The pipeline up to and including DEV is the team's; PROD is Operations.

03
The deployable artifact

A docker image built once and promoted up the chain (DEV → STAGE → PROD) without modification. k8s orchestration, container hygiene, repeatable deploys.

Operations runs PROD. AI Core remains a separate team and owns the agentic tooling layer; you integrate it into the developer and testing platforms.

§ 04

  • 3rd-level support on platform escalations
§ 05

Must-have

Startup DNA
Came up through tech product startups where you were one of fewer than ~20 engineers. Bonus if you were employee #1–10.
Jack of all trades
You've written backend services, set up CI, debugged a Kubernetes networking issue, written docs, run a workshop, and presented to a board, in the same year, without thinking it was unusual.
DevOps, hands-on
Personally built and run a production DevOps stack end-to-end. Not "led the team that did"; actually did.
10+ years engineering
Significant time in production operations. Not architecture-only.
Agentic, daily
Currently using and shipping with agentic coding tools. Daily work, not a curiosity.
Agile, introduced
Has introduced agile practices to a team that wasn't using them. Not "implemented scrum at a 200-engineer org through a consulting engagement"; actually got a small team running sprints that stuck.
Small-team lead
Has led a small engineering team (3–8) as a hands-on tech lead or hybrid manager.
In front of a room
Comfortable teaching, running workshops, giving honest feedback.
Mixed work over meetings
Pair with a junior in the morning, debug at lunch, present to a regulator's auditor in the afternoon. If that sounds like a great week, this is your job.

§ 07

What success looks like

30 days
Met every scrum team, the existing DevOps team, and the AI Core team. Shipped at least one concrete platform improvement (CI fix, observability default, eval template). Refocus plan locked. Team's first sprint underway. SHIP IT and k8s/containers project plans drafted.
90 days
Refocus complete: Engineering Catalysts running as a focused platform team. Team running in scrum. AI Core partnership rhythm established. SHIP IT and k8s/containers projects moving with clear milestones. Engineering Director and Architects supported with red team / certification tooling.
End of Phase 1 (6 months)
Platform measurably better (build times, dev environment reliability, deploy frequency DEV → STAGE). SHIP IT and k8s/containers in production. Phase 2 plan drafted with the Engineering Director.
